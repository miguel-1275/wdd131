Ecuador is the only country named after the equator line. Its Chimborazo volcano, though not the tallest from sea level, is the closest point to the sun from the Earth's center. Additionally, it is the most biodiverse country in the world per square kilometer, home to 16% of the planet's bird species. From the Galápagos Islands to the Amazon rainforest, Ecuador offers a unique natural diversity.
